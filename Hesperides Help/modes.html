Modes

What are modes ?

A mode is a component of Hesperides which explains how to different sounds are rendered in Tengwar. Sindarin and English, for example, are differently rendered in Tengwar.

Please remember that automated transcription is not always perfect, and that it sometimes fails to render some of the subtleties of J.R.R. Tolkien's writing system.

If you just want to transcribe Sindarin words, Hesperides includes two modes which are perfectly fit for the task ; the default settings will just work fine, but it you want to know what these modes are, read on.

Sindarin and Sindarin Classic

These modes are the two modes included in Hesperides. Sindarin was designed by Måns Björkman and is the mode used on the picture of the Doors of Moria included in the Lord of the Rings. Sindarin Classic was designed by René Vega and is the one used in the third version of the King's letter to Samwise Gamgee (found in the History of the Lord of the Rings, for example). It represents what men of Gondor would have written when trying to write Sindarin.

Other modes

Hesperides can use all the modes fit for Tengwar Scribe, a Windows transcription application. You can use, for example, all the modes here : just download the mode file, go to your Library folder (in your home directory), and open the Application Support folder ; drop the mode file in the Hesperides directory.
